Projections

This module explores the idea of projections. In particular, we consider the projection of 3-dimensional space onto a two-dimensional plane. Although, in general, we can project onto any plane that passes through the origin, in this module we will only consider the following planes: the \((x,y)\)-plane, the \((y,z)\)-plane, and the \((z,x)\)-plane. Projecting onto these planes is very convenient. For instance, projecting onto the \((x,y)\)-plane is the same as just setting the \(z\)-coordinate equal to 0.

In the diagrams your perspective is as follows:
  • In the leftmost diagram the direction from the screen up to your eye is the direction of the positive \(z\)-axis. The positive \(x\)-axis points to your right and the positive \(y\)-axis toward the top of your screen.
  • In the middle diagram the direction from the screen up to your eye is the direction of the positive \(x\)-axis. The positive \(y\)-axis points to your right and the positive \(z\)-axis toward the top of your screen.
  • In the rightmost diagram the direction from the screen up to your eye is the direction of the positive \(y\)-axis. The positive \(z\)-axis points to your right and the positive \(x\)-axis toward the top of your screen.

In this module you will be manipulating a vector that exists in 3-dimensional space. However, you will only be able to see its projections onto the \((x,y)\)-plane, the \((y,z)\)-plane, and the \((z,x)\)-plane, and will only be able to change it from one of these planes at a time.
