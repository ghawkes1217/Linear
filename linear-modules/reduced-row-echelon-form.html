Module 9: Reduced Row Echelon Form

Ever wondered if you could determine whether or not the inverse of a large square matrix, say $100\times 100$, exist? It turns out that you can do this in general, using the reduced row echelon form for matrices.

What exactly is the reduced row echelon form for a matrix?

For this module, we will begin with finding the reduced row echelon form (r.r.e.f.) for matrices. Next we use the r.r.e.f. to perform the following tasks:

  • Determine whether or not a $3\times 3$ matrix has an inverse, and if so, find its inverse.
  • Discover whether or not a variable in a system of equations can take any real value.
