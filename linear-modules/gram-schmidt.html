Module 17: Orthogonalization and Gram-Schmidt

In this module you will be given 3 vectors \(\textcolor{red}{u}\), \(\textcolor{green}{v}\), \(\textcolor{blue}{w}\). Your goal will be to a new set of vectors \(\textcolor{red}{U}\), \(\textcolor{green}{V}\), \(\textcolor{blue}{W}\) with the following properties:

  • \(\textcolor{red}{U}\) and \(\textcolor{green}{V}\) and \(\textcolor{blue}{W}\) are unit vectors
  • We have \(\textcolor{red}{U} \cdot \textcolor{green}{V}=0\), and \(\textcolor{green}{V} \cdot \textcolor{blue}{W}=0\), and \(\textcolor{red}{U} \cdot \textcolor{blue}{W}=0\),
  • We have \(\langle \textcolor{red}{u} \rangle= \langle \textcolor{red}{U} \rangle \), and \(\langle \textcolor{red}{u}, \textcolor{green}{v} \rangle= \langle \textcolor{red}{U}, \textcolor{green}{V} \rangle \), and \(\langle \textcolor{red}{u}, \textcolor{green}{v}, \textcolor{blue}{w} \rangle= \langle \textcolor{red}{U}, \textcolor{green}{V}, \textcolor{blue}{W} \rangle \),
Where the notation \( \langle \rangle \) signifies the vector space of all linear combinations of vectors in brackets.

In order to achieve this goal you will follow these steps:

  • Scale the vector \( \textcolor{red}{u} \) until it has unit length (take this to be \( \textcolor{red}{U} \))
  • Project \( \textcolor{green}{v} \) onto \( \textcolor{red}{U} \) and substract the result from \( \textcolor{green}{v} \). Then scale the result until it has unit length. This will be \( \textcolor{green}{V} \).
  • Project \( \textcolor{blue}{w} \) onto \( \textcolor{red}{U} \) and substract the result from \( \textcolor{blue}{w} \). Then project the new \(\textcolor{blue}{w}\) onto \( \textcolor{green}{V} \) and substract the result from \(\textcolor{blue}{w}\). Finally, scale \(\textcolor{blue}{w}\) until it is a unit vector. The result is \(\textcolor{blue}{W}\).
This process of changing a basis of vectors into a new basis that is orthonormal (all vectors have unit length and any two are perpendicular to each other) is known as the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process. Note that in the process, you never need to consider more than two vectors at any one time. In fact, in the interactive below you will only be able to view two vectors at a time:
  • Click the "red/green" button to see a representation of the plane spanned by \( \textcolor{red}{u} \) and \( \textcolor{green}{v} \) and manipulate these vectors
  • Click the "green/blue" button to see a representation of the plane spanned by \( \textcolor{green}{v}\) and \( \textcolor{blue}{w} \) and manipulate these vectors
  • Click the "red/blue" button to see a representation of the plane spanned by \( \textcolor{red}{u} \) and \( \textcolor{blue}{w}\) and manipulate these vectors
